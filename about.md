## [[alternative reality]]: JAM!

A game jam from March 21-25 to build an [[alternative reality]] game.

Wait, do you mean augmented reality (AR)? Nope!

Do you mean alternate reality game (ARG)? Nope! Well, kind of Alternative, as in alternative tech, alternative media, alternative infrastructure: another, perhaps more radical way of engaging with what's already here. We're inspired by practices including alternate reality games, pervasive games, urban games, LARP, immersive theater, etc,. but we're not precious about genre. The goal is to design playful experiences that combine game mechanics with the real world. No-tech, low-tech, or high-tech all welcome.

## FAQ:
What counts as an [[alternative reality]] game?
Our definition might be broader than the traditional definition of an “ARG”- any experience that combines game mechanisms and elements in the real-world as part of its design. Think GPS, maps, websites, social media, texting, physical locations, role-playing, puzzles, public transport, parcels, QR codes, city infrastructures, etc. 

What tools/skills do I need?
Be no-tech, low-tech or high-tech. You might use printed materials, websites, social media accounts, online forums, google calendar, NFC tags, audio recordings...everything honestly, or just clever use of public spaces.
         
How do I participate if I’m not in a node city?
You can make and play games from anywhere, and submit directly via itch.io! If you’d like to find a team or a collaborator (in your city or across timezones), see question below.

Can I work alone/in teams? Where can I find teammates?
We host matchmaking in our discord, and during irl node events. We do have a faint hope that people would form teams across countries, and create games that has to be completed by both cities together, but working solo is equally welcome!

What format should my game submission be?
Your submission on itch.io should include:
A game page with a clear description of your game and what players need, including materials, number of players, approximate playtime, etc.
Any necessary files or links
A play mode declaration (see "What happens after submission?" below)
We highly encourage games that are "pick up and play", meaning that it’s ready with rules, manuals, and materials that people could participate without you being present. That said, games requiring a game master or facilitator presence are also welcome.

What happens after submission? Will my game be played?
That depends! You can select one of these play modes upon submission:
Self-organized: You run your own game! You recruit players via Discord and organize your own sessions.
Open pickup: Your game needs to be facilitated, but it’s documented clearly enough and you’d like for someone to pick it up and run it without you. It goes into a browsable pool during PLAY! weekend and player groups can grab it themselves. Works best for games that don't require designer to be present and not very location specific.
Open play: Your game is finished and can be accessed and played without facilitation. 
If your game was made at an IRL node gathering, your local organizer will decide whether and how to include your game in their local PLAY! session. 
All submissions will be included in our web archive, regardless of whether or it went through the PLAY! weekend.

What if I can’t finish my game?
If your game isn't finished by PLAY! weekend, you can still submit later and self-organize play sessions on your own timeline. Unfinished work and prototypes are also welcome in the archive.

Can I participate in PLAY! without joining JAM!?
Yes. PLAY! is open to everyone. Details on how to access games coming closer to March 28.

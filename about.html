about

You are in an [[alternative reality]] game. You have been playing it your whole life. It has weather, people, rankings, transit systems of varying reliability... and no clear win condition. it has been extremely online for the past couple seasons.

[[alternative reality]] conference is an event that takes this situation seriously. For two weeks in March, game creators across time-zones gather to make [[alternative reality]] games that spill out of the screen and into the world.

Initiated by Shanghai-based translocal collective [[rect*]]repair. Supported by morning.fyi. Created by your participation.

"Do you mean alternate reality game (ARG)?"
Nope!

...Well, kind of! Alternative, as in alternative tech, alternative media, alternative infrastructure: another, perhaps more radical way of engaging with what's already here. We're inspired by practices including alternate reality games, pervasive games, urban games, LARP, immersive theater, etc., but we're not precious about genre.

The game jam is from March 21–25, with a kickoff event on Saturday, March 21st. The goal is to design playful experiences that combine game mechanics with the real world. No-tech, low-tech, or high-tech all welcome.

FAQ

What counts as an [[alternative reality]] game?

Our definition might be broader than the traditional definition of an "ARG"—any experience that combines game mechanisms and elements in the real world as part of its design. Think GPS, maps, websites, social media, texting, physical locations, role-playing, puzzles, public transport, parcels, QR codes, city infrastructures, etc.

What tools/skills do I need?

No-tech, low-tech, or high-tech all welcome. You might use printed materials, websites, social media accounts, online forums, Google Calendar, NFC tags, audio recordings... everything, or just clever use of public spaces.

How do I participate if I'm not in a node city?

You can make and play games from anywhere and submit directly via itch.io! If you'd like to find a team or collaborator (in your city or across timezones), join the Discord.

Can I work alone or in teams? Where can I find teammates?

We host matchmaking in our Discord and during IRL node events. We faintly hope people would form teams across countries and create games that have to be completed by both cities together—but working solo is equally welcome!

What format should my game submission be?

Your submission on itch.io should include: a game page with a clear description and what players need (materials, number of players, approximate playtime, etc.), any necessary files or links, and a play mode declaration. We highly encourage "pick up and play" games—ready with rules, manuals, and materials that people could participate without you being present. That said, games requiring a game master or facilitator are also welcome.

What happens after submission? Will my game be played?

That depends! You can select one of these play modes upon submission:

  • Self-organized: You run your own game and recruit players via Discord.
  • Open pickup: Your game is documented clearly enough for someone to pick it up and run it without you.
  • Open play: Your game can be accessed and played without facilitation.

All submissions will be included in our web archive, regardless of whether they went through the PLAY! weekend.

What if I can't finish my game?

If your game isn't finished by PLAY! weekend, you can still submit later and self-organize play sessions on your own timeline. Unfinished work and prototypes are also welcome in the archive.

Can I participate in PLAY! without joining JAM!?

Yes. PLAY! is open to everyone. Details on how to access games coming closer to March 28.
